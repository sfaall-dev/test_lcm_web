Improvement of soil phosphorus utilization for Biological Nitrogen Fixation (BNF) in the soils of the Senegalese groundnut basin: taking into account the agro-ecological dimension of BNF in food legume crops (2021 - 2022)

Senegal, like other sub-Saharan African countries, must ensure its self-sufficiency and food security through a substantial increase in agricultural production, but also face the continuous degradation of production systems (especially soils). To provide solutions, it is obvious that sustainable innovations must be proposed. The new paradigm shift in the new green revolution strongly advocates increasing smart agro-ecological practices that imitate natural ecological processes. This research project aims to develop sustainable agro-ecological practices using microbial inoculants as plant bio-fortifiers to increase phosphorus utilization efficiency and ensure efficient biological nitrogen fixation (BNF) in a model plant, the groundnut. To do this, the best strains of rhizobia, arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi and PGPR (Plant Growth Promoting Rhizobacteria) from the collections of the Joint Microbiology Laboratory IRD/ISRA/UCAD and the Dakar Mushroom Biotechnology Laboratory will be selected for their ability to promote phosphorus solubilization and optimize BNF. Symbiotic association tests will be carried out under laboratory and greenhouse conditions on groundnut varieties 55437, Fleur 11 and 69101 with the selected strains, in order to develop high-performance inocula and develop fertilizing bioproducts easy to integrate into peasant agricultural practices. These fertilizing bioproducts will be tested in three agro-ecological zones of the groundnut basin. Their impacts on groundnut productivity as well as on the physico-chemical properties and biological quality of the soils will be evaluated. This multidisciplinary research project is a partnership between UCAD, ISRA, IRD, USSEIN and the OPs. The results obtained will contribute to an improvement in groundnut productivity and soil fertility, allowing to secure producers' incomes and fight poverty.

Funder(s): Jointly funded by the Ministry of Higher Education, Research and Innovation (MESRI) of Senegal and the International Development Research Centre (IDRC). SGCI2-IDRC-MESRI

Restoration of the fertility of degraded arable lands in Senegal by integrating Casuarinaceae nitrogen-fixing trees into agricultural crops (2021-2024)

Land degradation is a global environmental issue with devastating consequences for food production and human well-being. Senegal faces a serious problem of arable land degradation due, among other things, to long periods of monoculture with no fallows and the strong anthropogenic pressures on natural resources. Given that arable land represents only 19% of the national territory and that the Senegalese population has almost tripled in the space of 50 years, there is an urgent need to restore the fertility of these degraded soils and ensure livelihood security in rural areas. Agroforestry practices in which nitrogen-fixing trees are integrated into agricultural crops are one of the sustainable strategies to restore the fertility of degraded land. Fast-growing nitrogen-fixing trees belonging to the Casuarinaceae family are pioneer plants, able to colonize disturbed soils and increase their fertility, thanks to their capacity to establish symbiotic associations both with mycorrhizal fungi (arbuscular and ectomycorrhizal) and the nitrogen-fixing bacteria Frankia. In Senegal, Casuarinaceae are mainly used for sand dunes stabilization and the rehabilitation of salt-degraded land. In this project, we will: (i) study the effect of Casuarinaceae-crops intercropping on the growth and productivity of associated agricultural crops, (ii) evaluate the impact of Casuarinaceae-crops intercropping on the physico-chemical properties and microbiome of the soil and (iii) assess the effect of Casuarinaceae-crops intercropping on the biological quality of the soil using nematodes as bio-indicators. This research will contribute to a better understanding of the effects of the use of Casuarinaceae in agroforestry on crop productivity and soil fertility.

Comparative metagenomics of the peanut rhizomicrobiome Arachis hypogaea L. for the development of next-generation biofertilizers (2021-2022)

This research project aims to develop sustainable agro-ecological practices using microbial inoculants as plant bio-fortifiers to increase phosphorus utilization efficiency and ensure efficient biological nitrogen fixation (BNF) in a model plant, the groundnut. To do this, the comparative metagenomics of the rhizomicrobiome of the main peanut varieties cultivated in Senegal will be carried out in order to identify the functional groups associated with them and define microbial inoculants fertilizing according to varieties, physico-chemical characteristics of soils and agro-ecological zones of the peanut production basin in Senegal. The best strains identified at each functional group (rhizobia, arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi and PGPR-Plant Growth Promoting Rhizobacteria) from the collections of the Joint Microbiology Laboratory (LCM) IRD/ISRA/UCAD and the Mushroom Biotechnology Laboratory (LBC) of Dakar will be selected for their ability to promote phosphorus solubilization and optimize BNF in peanuts. Symbiotic association tests will be carried out in laboratory and greenhouse to produce high-performance inocula and develop fertilizing bioproducts easy to integrate into peasant agricultural practices. This multidisciplinary research project is a partnership between UCAD, IRD, ISRA (LNRPV and CNRA), USSEIN and OPs. The results obtained will contribute to an improvement in peanut productivity and soil fertility, allowing to secure producers' incomes and fight poverty.

Funder: Internal Research Support Fund of UCAD

Coordinator: Dr G. SENE / email: godar.sene@ucad.edu.sn
