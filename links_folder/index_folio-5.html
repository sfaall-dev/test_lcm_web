1. The Quality Management System

The LCM is the first microbiology laboratory of soils in Africa to be certified to ISO 9001 version 2008 standard. This certification follows an initiative undertaken since 2005 and was awarded after an audit by EQS (Euro Quality System). It is an international recognition and reflects the LCM's commitment to excellence while valorizing the scientific work and student training carried out within the LCM.

1.1 - Extract from the LCM Quality Policy Declaration

Our quality charter is part of IRD's quality policy dated November 6, 2017 and demonstrates our commitment to meet the needs of all interested parties and ensure their satisfaction.

Currently, the challenges of the Joint Microbiology Laboratory (LCM) are multiple and are expressed externally in terms of:

  • - National and international reputation of the laboratory;
  • - Model of successful cooperation: North-South and South-South;
  • - Contribution to achieving food security in Senegal in a context of sustainable agricultural production.

Internally, the challenges of the laboratory are expressed in terms of:
  • - Increased scientific and societal valorization of research results;
  • - Sustained acquisition of funding from institutional and financial partners;
  • - Opening towards new disciplines (bioinformatics, genetics...)
  • - Production of scientific knowledge but also knowledge transfer and management of biological resources.

The beneficiaries, potential users of our research are Educational and/or Research Institutions, students, partners working in the field of environment and agriculture. Therefore, the Quality Approach we adopt will be based on continuous training of personnel in methods and continuous improvement tools referring to ISO 9001: 2015 standard. This will consist of:

  • - Defining the functional organization of the laboratory, responsibilities, methods and means used;
  • Developing annual plans for quality improvement.

Stressing that the motivation of each person regardless of their position is necessary to implement and sustain the process of continuous improvement of the Quality System, we personally commit to:

  • Informing staff at all levels;
  • Stimulate staff motivation and encourage training;
  • Ensure the quality system is applied at all levels;
  • Comply with legal and regulatory requirements;
  • Monitor health and safety of staff;
  • Implement the necessary means in terms of both material and human resources;
  • Create and maintain an atmosphere of dialogue and fulfillment for all.
1.2 - Certified Scope

The scope of this approach concerns the following activities:

  • Culture, isolation and molecular characterization of rhizobia,
  • Conservation and distribution of microorganisms for use by the scientific community,
  • Culture and inoculation of mycorrhizal fungi
  • Reception and training, provision of technical means and expertise for the implementation of scientific programs.
